Short Bio

I am a second year PhD student affiliated with the Information Processing Group (IPG) in the School of Computer and Communication Sciences at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland. I am fortunate to be co-advised by Yanina Shkel and Michael Gastpar, and I broadly work in Information and Coding Theory.

Before this, I did my undergraduate with a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Patna (IIT-P) in India. I have been extremely fortunate to have worked with Sidharth Jaggi (University of Bristol), Amitalok Budkuley (IIT Kharagpur), Yihan Zhang (IST Austria), Laura Luzzi (ENSEA, CNRS), and Preetam Kumar (IIT Patna) during my undergraduate degree.

Research Interests

My specific Research Interests include:

  • Information & Coding Theory

  • Security, Privacy & Cryptography

  • Machine Learning

  • Computational Genomics

Currently, I am working on the Minimum Entropy Couplings and the Change-point detection problem under privacy. Additionally, I frequently read articles on Game theory.
